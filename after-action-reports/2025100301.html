Detailed Watering Instructions — 4,600 sq ft, mixed sun & shade (Edmond, OK)

Context: October in Edmond typically shows cooling daytime highs (mid/upper 60s–70s°F) and cooler nights; precipitation is variable. Because nights are cooler and humidity modest, disease pressure rises if turf stays wet overnight — water early in the day. Sources: Oklahoma State Univ. Extension (watering guidance) and local climate summaries.

Immediate post-work (Day 0 — same afternoon as seeding)

  • Do not flood. After finishing seed & topdress, perform a light initial wetting: apply just enough water to moisten the top ½ inch of soil (this encourages seed imbibition). For your lawn that’s approximately 0.05–0.10 inch (roughly 287–287 × 0.1 = 286.6 gallons for 0.1" across the whole lawn). Use short runs on sprinkler zones to test depth (see measurement method below).
  • Start the formal schedule the next morning (see Day 1–14 below).

Establishment schedule (Days 1–14) — frequent, light irrigation

  • Goal: Keep the seedbed surface consistently damp but not waterlogged so seeds can germinate. Avoid puddling and avoid long leaf wetness overnight.
  • Frequency: 2–3 times per day (early morning, mid/late morning, and optionally early afternoon if hot & windy). Reduce midday watering if skies are cool/cloudy or if rainfall occurs.
  • Per event target: 0.05–0.12 inch per event (this wets the top ¼–½" without deep percolation). For the entire lawn that equals roughly 287–344 gallons per event (0.1" ≈ 286.6 gal; 0.12" ≈ 343.9 gal). Adjust by sprinkler output (see measurement method below).
  • Typical timing: 6:00–8:30 AM (first), 10:30–11:30 AM (second), optional 2:00–3:30 PM (third) — avoid late afternoon/evening irrigation to reduce overnight leaf wetness.

Transition schedule (Days 15–42) — taper to deeper, less frequent irrigation

  • As seedlings germinate and traffic is still restricted, move to once-daily deeper watering for ~1 week, then 2–3 times per week deeper waterings.
  • Per deep watering: 0.5–0.75 inch per session (encourages roots to grow deeper). For your area that equals approximately 1,432.9 gallons (0.5") to 2,149.4 gallons (0.75") per watering across the whole lawn.
  • Space deep waterings 48–72 hours apart, adjust depending on weather/rainfall.

Established lawn maintenance (after ~6–8 weeks)

  • Target 1.0–1.5 inches per week of water in mixed sun/shade (tall fescue prefers the higher end in full sun). For 4,600 sq ft, 1.0" ≈ 2,865.8 gallons per week; 1.5" ≈ 4,298.7 gallons per week. Adjust by shade and soil type.
  • Prefer early morning irrigation (before 10 AM) to reduce disease risk and evaporation.
Rain & local weather adjustments: Before following the schedule check forecast. If forecast shows ≥0.25–0.5" rain within 24 hours, reduce or skip irrigation. October in Edmond can deliver some rain events — use them to reduce watering.

How to measure sprinkler output (quick field test — do this once per zone)

  1. Place several identical straight-sided containers (tin cans, cat food cans) spaced across the zone to be tested.
  2. Run the zone for exactly 15 minutes.
  3. Measure depth of water in each can (in inches). Average the cans to get inches per 15 minutes.
  4. Use that rate to calculate minutes per event for target inches. Example: if your zone puts out 0.08" in 15 minutes, to deliver 0.10" you'd run ≈ (0.10 / 0.08) × 15 ≈ 19 minutes.

Special notes for mixed sun & shade

  • Shade areas (near large trees or north-facing structures) retain moisture longer — reduce frequency or volume there to avoid disease. Consider hand-watering or smaller zone times for shaded strips.
  • Sunny broad areas need the higher weekly totals (toward 1.5" / week) to avoid drought stress.

Pest / disease & safety reminders

  • Water early in the day (before 10 AM) to minimize leaf wetness overnight and lower fungal disease risk.
  • If you see standing water/puddles, reduce event volume and increase run frequency shorter times (to improve infiltration) or check sprinkler coverage.
  • Document all irrigation adjustments and rainfall for client records.
