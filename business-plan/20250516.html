Abundant Life Land and Tree LLC - Updated Business Plan (2025 Edition)

1. Executive Summary

Business Name: Abundant Life Land and Tree LLC
Owner: Christian Castillo
Location: Edmond, Oklahoma
Website: www.abundantlife.land
Established: January 2025

Mission: To provide land stewardship services that restore beauty, function, and biodiversity to suburban environments while promoting sustainability and community wellness.

Vision: To become the most trusted, earth-conscious tree and land care provider in Edmond and surrounding areas, empowering families to reconnect with the land.

2. Services Offered

Tree Care

  • Pruning (health, structure, clearance, view)
  • Small to mid-size removals
  • Storm cleanup
  • Risk assessment and reporting (ISA certification in progress)

Landscaping

  • Flower bed design & installation
  • Soil amendments & mulch delivery
  • Seasonal cleanups
  • Edging, weeding, and maintenance

Eco-Lawn Care

  • Electric push mowing (low noise/emissions)
  • Trimming & edging
  • Leaf mulching & composting options

Specialty Services

  • Pollinator-friendly garden installs
  • Native/low-water plantings
  • Litter/environmental cleanup
  • Consulting on sustainable land use

3. Target Market

Primary Audience:

  • Homeowners in Edmond, OK (especially upper-middle-class women aged 35–55)
  • Environmentally conscious families
  • Busy professionals seeking boutique-level service

Secondary Audience:

  • Local churches, schools, and HOAs
  • Elderly homeowners needing reliable outdoor care

4. Competitive Advantage

  • Personalized boutique experience
  • Veteran-owned, family-operated
  • Faith-integrated business values
  • Quiet, clean electric equipment
  • Growing tree knowledge and certification
  • Strong branding and online presence

5. Marketing Strategy

  • SEO-optimized website with content on tree and eco-landscaping
  • Instagram & Facebook with testimonials and visuals
  • Local bulletin boards and branded signage
  • Referral discounts
  • Community workshops and speaking gigs

6. Operations Plan

  • Solo operator with part-time help as needed
  • Prioritize 4–6 jobs/week, tree work preferred
  • Chevy Silverado 2500HD, electric Milwaukee M18 tools
  • Rental gear used until replaced with owned gear
  • Workflow: Estimate → Proposal → Job → Follow-up → Upsell

7. Financial Plan

Revenue Goals:

  • $4,000–$6,000/month by Fall 2025
  • Focus on $1,000+ tree and install jobs

Expenses:

  • New equipment
  • Truck repairs/maintenance
  • Insurance
  • Marketing & signage

Profitability Strategy:

  • Efficient solo operation
  • Focus on high-margin services
  • Create recurring plans for income stability
