Plant Gallery

Woodland Phlox (Phlox divaricata)

  • Reaches 8 to 12 inches tall and spreads gently by rhizomes.
  • Produces lavender-blue flowers from March through May.
  • Lives 3 to 4 years but spreads to maintain its presence.

Wild Columbine (Aquilegia Canadensis)

  • Reaches about 1-3 feet tall with delicate foliage and blooms.
  • Red and yellow flowers bloom from March through May.
  • Lives 3 to 5 years but readily self-seeds for longevity.

Bugleherb (Ajuga Reptans)

  • Grows only 4-6 inches tall and spreads quickly as groundcover.
  • Spikes of blue-purple flowers bloom from April through May.
  • Can live for many years when kept in check and healthy.

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea Purpurea)

  • Grows to about 2-4 feet in full sun or part shade.
  • Blooms heavily from June to August attracting pollinators.
  • Can persist for 4 to 6 years, longer if self-seeded.

Wild Bergamot (Monarda Fistulosa)

  • Grows 2-4 feet tall with square stems and minty scent.
  • Blooms with lavender flowers from June through August.
  • Lives 3 to 5 years, spreads by rhizomes and reseeding.

Little Bluestem (Schizachyrium Scoparium)

  • Grows 2-4 feet tall and adds vertical visual interest.
  • Blooms August through October with feathery seed heads.
  • This native grass typically lives 5 to 7 years in gardens.

Aromatic Aster (Symphyotrichum Oblongifolium)

  • Compact size of 1-2 feet makes it a great front border.
  • Blooms profusely from September into early November.
  • Can thrive for 5 to 8 years in low-maintenance beds.

Dwarf Yaupon Holly

  • Matures at about 3-5 feet tall with a dense shape.
  • Blooms with tiny white flowers from March to April.
  • Can live for decades, often exceeding 20 to 30 years.

Soft Caress (Mahonia Eurybracteata)

  • This evergreen shrub reaches 3-4 feet at full maturity.
  • Produces delicate yellow blooms from October to December each year.
  • Thrives for over 10 years with proper care and placement.

Care Tips

  • Water weekly through establishment, then only during prolonged dry spells.
  • Use cedar mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds, but leave space around stems.
  • Deadhead coneflower, aster, and bergamot to extend bloom time.
  • Let phlox and ajuga form natural colonies under the tree.
