Shari's Stewardship Plan

Abundant Life Land & Tree

This plan reflects a 5-month seasonal journey through land restoration, planting, and system establishment, customized for your property.

Onboarding Notes

  • Bulk of work to be delayed 1 month for hardscaping projects completion.
  • Bur oak planting proposed at northwest corner (NW adjacent to former elm site)
  • Cherry tree planting to add flowering, fruiting, year round interest.
  • Soil amendments applied to detoxify effects of long-term herbicide/pesticide use
  • Beneficial nematodes applied for grub control
  • Electric mowing declined to preserve current provider relationship

Monthly Care Calendar

🌱 June – “Sprout” (2 hrs)

  • Discuss client goals, develop monthly calendar for the year
  • Assess current light, soil, and moisture conditions
  • Evaluate invasive species presence and develop management plan
  • Nandina shrub removal (invasive, toxic to birds, spreads aggressively)

🌳 July – “Forest” (16 hrs)

  • Tree sourcing (pricing, inspection, pickup)
  • Install bur oak and cherry trees with companion understory plantings
  • Order soil testing kit, collect sample, interpret results
  • Apply compost and microbially rich soil amendments
  • Deep mulching and water basin formation for new installs
  • Invasive species active management, manual removal

🌲 August – “Canopy” (12 hrs)

  • Install beneficial nematodes for grub suppression
  • Begin installing perennial borders (native flowers and grasses)
  • Broad-scale soil inoculation (biochar, compost tea, fungal spores)
  • Ongoing tree and shrub care, watering checks, and soil support
  • Seasonal pruning of summer growth
  • Seed selection and ordering for fall

🌿 September – “Sapling” (8 hrs)

  • Plant fall-interest perennials and shrubs
  • Decorative pollinator shelter additions (framed habitat piles, etc.)
  • Second round of soil enrichment and compost layering

🍁 October – “Seedling” (4 hrs)

  • Prepare areas for winter rest: chop & drop, cover cropping
  • Mulch refresh and winter root protection
  • Soil test for next year’s amendment plan
  • Client walkthrough and planning notes for spring
