Climber Preparation SOP

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the required steps before any climber leaves the ground. Preparation prevents emergencies.

1. Job & Tree Assessment

  • Confirm work scope and objectives
  • Identify tree species and structural condition
  • Assess decay, cracks, deadwood, and hangers
  • Identify utilities, targets, and exclusion zones
  • Confirm weather conditions are acceptable

2. Climber Personal Readiness

  • Proper PPE worn and inspected
  • Hydrated, rested, and physically ready
  • No impairment from illness, fatigue, or substances
  • Mental readiness confirmed

3. Gear Inspection & Setup

  • Harness, bridge, and attachments inspected
  • Climbing line inspected and correctly installed
  • Secondary system (lanyard) functional
  • Connectors locked and oriented correctly
  • Friction devices functioning properly

4. Tool Preparation

  • Saws inspected, fueled, and sharp
  • Chains properly tensioned
  • Tool lanyards installed
  • Handsaws secured
  • First cut plan confirmed

5. Communication & Ground Coordination

  • Primary and backup ground contact identified
  • Verbal commands reviewed
  • Hand signals reviewed if needed
  • Emergency response plan reviewed
  • Drop zones clearly established

6. Final Go / No-Go Check

  • All crew clear and attentive
  • Ropes routed cleanly and protected
  • No unresolved hazards
  • Climber verbally confirms readiness
  • Ground lead acknowledges

A calm, deliberate start leads to a safe and efficient climb. Never rush preparation.
