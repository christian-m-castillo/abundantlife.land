Purpose: This agreement establishes a single-point-of-contact stewardship framework for 12804 Plum Hollow Drive. The intent is to manage the property as a unified living system: residence, lawn, meadow, woodland, wildlife habitat, garden spaces, and mature canopy.

The work is not best approached as isolated landscaping tasks. It is better approached as continuing property stewardship: regular observation, routine care, prioritized risk reduction, and phased improvements over time.

Stewardship Role

Abundant Life Land & Tree LLC will serve as the primary steward for the managed exterior portions of the property, providing routine care, arboricultural work, meadow maintenance, woodland observation, project planning, and phased implementation.

Highly technical horticulture, arboriculture, and silviculture will be performed directly by the owner. Support work and supervised technical work may be performed by properly trained workers under owner direction.

Because the property contains mature trees, active habitat, slope, meadow, and woodland edge, regular presence on site is valuable. Routine maintenance visits allow small issues to be observed before they become larger failures or missed opportunities.

Pricing Framework

The following formula keeps the relationship transparent and scalable. Each task is priced according to actual labor category, time required, materials, disposal, and direct project costs.

Category Rate / Formula Applies To
Ground Operations $2.00 per man-minute Mowing, trimming, edging, hauling, ground pruning, cleanup, dragging brush, loading, transplanting, garden work, meadow work, and general stewardship labor.
Technical Arboriculture $3.00 per technical minute Specialized pruning, climbing, tree assessment, canopy work, structural pruning decisions, and technical arboricultural judgment.
High-Risk Technical Arboriculture $4.00 per technical minute Elevated-risk climbing, rigging over targets, compromised trees, storm-damaged trees, hollow/decayed stems, spar work, and removals where failure consequences are higher.
Support Work & Supervised Technical Work $2.00 per man-minute Ground support, hauling, cleanup, brush management, rope handling, planting, meadow work, and supervised technical work performed by properly trained workers under owner direction.

Rental Equipment Direct rental cost × 1.5 + operating labor Trailers, lifts, stump grinders, loaders, log splitters, trenchers, chippers, specialty saws, compact equipment, or other project-specific rented equipment.
Deposits Materials/rentals in advance + scheduled labor reserve Projects requiring purchased materials, plant orders, rental equipment, or reserved workdays may require a deposit before scheduling or procurement.
Offsite Hauling & Disposal Labor + disposal fees + load charge Brush, logs, sod, soil, invasive plant material, removed landscape debris, and any material hauled away from the property.
Technical Arboriculture Formula

Total = Climber technical minutes × applicable technical rate + (support work minutes × $2.00) + hauling/disposal + materials or direct costs.

Highly technical horticulture, arboriculture, and silviculture decisions and execution will be performed directly by owner. Support work and supervised technical work may be performed by properly trained workers under owner direction.

Material Handling, Woodland Integration, and Haul-Off

Preferred approach: Where appropriate, logs, brush, chips, and organic matter may be integrated back into the woodland as habitat structure, erosion control, natural edging, nurse logs, mulch, or future soil-building material.

Offsite haul-off formula: Ground labor minutes × $2.00 + actual disposal fees + load charge.

Load charge: $95 per pickup load, $250 per trailer load, or actual rental/fuel/disposal-cycle cost if higher.

Onsite reuse reduces disposal cost and can improve habitat value. Offsite hauling is used when material is unsuitable for reuse, excessive in volume, diseased, invasive, unsightly, or otherwise requested to be removed.

Rental Equipment Formula

Total = Direct rental cost × 1.5 + pickup/return labor + operating labor + delivery/fuel/damage waiver fees where applicable.

Rental equipment is used only when it improves safety, efficiency, access, or quality of work enough to justify the additional cost.

Deposit Formula

Materials or rentals: 100% of expected materials, plant orders, rentals, delivery fees, and special-order costs are due before procurement.

Reserved project labor: For larger scheduled projects, a labor reserve of 25%–50% of the estimated labor may be requested to hold the work window.

Deposits are credited toward the final invoice. Final balance is due upon substantial completion unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Initial Scope of Full Stewardship

1. Front Estate Grounds

Routine electric maintenance of the front grounds to preserve a clean, intentional appearance while minimizing noise, exhaust, and disturbance.

  • Electric mowing
  • String trimming
  • Edging along hardscape and bed lines
  • Hard-surface cleanup
  • Observation of turf, shrubs, irrigation effects, drainage, weeds, and developing issues
Recurring Front Grounds Pricing

Formula: Ground Operations Minutes × $2.00

Example: 75 minutes of solo electric care = 75 × $2.00 = $150 per visit.

Actual visit time may change seasonally with growth rate, edging needs, moisture, leaf fall, and property conditions.

2. Meadow Stewardship

The meadow should not be managed as ordinary turf. It is a living ecological zone that benefits from selective maintenance rather than uniform mowing.

  • Selective mowing or trimming according to seasonal conditions
  • Walking path maintenance
  • Suppression of invasive or undesirable species
  • Retention of beneficial habitat structure
  • Observation of erosion, compaction, drainage, wildlife activity, and desirable volunteer species
  • Coordination with future meadow and pollinator development
Meadow Stewardship Pricing

Formula: Ground Operations Minutes × $2.00 + disposal or materials where applicable.

Meadow work is best priced by actual time because the work varies with season, growth, rainfall, access, and ecological objectives.

3. Woodland Stewardship

Regular woodland observation allows tree health, deadwood, wildlife habitat, slope conditions, invasive plants, and future use areas to be managed intentionally.

  • Tree health observation
  • Hazard recognition and prioritization
  • Deadwood monitoring
  • Volunteer tree selection and removal recommendations
  • Woodland edge management
  • Guidance for future trails, gathering spaces, and patio integration

4. Garden and Habitat Development

Future improvements can be implemented gradually under the same pricing framework.

  • Raised vegetable garden construction
  • Pollinator and wildlife plantings
  • Beautyberry and native shrub integration
  • Woodland understory development
  • Transplanting desirable plants where practical
  • Mulch, compost, soil, edging, and hard-good installation
Installation Pricing

Total = Labor minutes by category + materials × 2.7 + rental equipment formula + haul-off formula where required.

Priority Arboricultural Work

Note on technical work: Highly technical horticulture, arboriculture, and silviculture will be performed directly by Christian. Support work and supervised technical work may be performed by properly trained workers under owner direction. Pricing and planning are therefore based on Christian's technical minutes plus support labor where appropriate, rather than assuming multiple independent technical climbers.

Hazard Hackberry Removal Priority Safety Work

Reason: Large mature hackberry with significant structural concerns, including basal compromise and lightning-related damage. This is the most consequential known tree risk on the property.

Scope:

  • Set climbing access and work positioning systems
  • Dismantle crown and stem in manageable sections
  • Rig or control pieces as required to protect fence, adjacent trees, slope, and woodland features
  • Ground crew brush handling, log movement, site organization, and cleanup
  • Material staged onsite for beneficial reuse or hauled offsite by agreement

Pricing Formula: High-risk technical minutes × $4.00 + support work minutes × $2.00 + hauling/disposal + special equipment or direct costs.

Final work plan should be confirmed after discussing desired level of onsite wood retention versus offsite removal.

Declining Lacebark Elm Removal Priority Safety Work

Reason: Severe decline and structural concern above or near an outdoor living area.

Scope:

  • Remove declining tree to appropriate height or grade as selected
  • Control pieces based on targets and access
  • Process brush and wood for onsite retention or offsite disposal
  • Clean work area

Pricing Formula: Ground Operations Minutes × $2.00 and/or Technical minutes × $4.00 where aerial positioning or technical arboricultural judgment is required + hauling/disposal.

Black Walnut House Clearance Pruning Preservation Work

Reason: Preserve the mature black walnut while reducing conflict with the house and roofline.

Scope:

  • Selective pruning for clearance
  • Reduction of roof and siding conflicts
  • Deadwood removal where accessible and appropriate
  • Conservative pruning cuts to preserve structure and health
  • Root conflicts evaluated conservatively and addressed only where justified

Pricing Formula: Technical minutes × $3.00 + support work minutes × $2.00 + cleanup/disposal as selected.

Woodland Hazard Mitigation Access Preparation

Reason: Future woodland access and gathering areas should begin with removal or reduction of obvious deadwood and foreseeable hazards.

Scope:

  • Walk-through hazard review
  • Removal of reachable deadwood and failed limbs
  • Priority marking for future aerial work
  • Selective cleanup near intended access routes and gathering areas

Pricing Formula: Ground Operations Minutes × $2.00 + Technical minutes × $3.00 or $4.00 only where technical aerial or high-risk work is required.

Service Agreement Structure

1. Stewardship Relationship

Confirm that Abundant Life Land & Tree LLC will serve as the primary point of contact for exterior stewardship, including front grounds, meadow, woodland, tree care, garden planning, and phased improvements.

2. Routine Grounds and Meadow Care

Routine presence establishes rhythm and allows ongoing observation. Front electric lawn care and meadow stewardship can begin immediately under the ground operations formula.

3. Priority Safety Work

Prioritize the hazard hackberry, declining lacebark elm, black walnut clearance, and woodland hazard mitigation. Work may be staged according to weather, crew availability, equipment needs, and desired handling of debris and wood.

4. Reassessment and Next Phase Planning

After major safety work is complete, the property should be reassessed for access, light, views, usable space, and ecological opportunities. This will guide the next phase of pruning, transplanting, planting, trail work, and garden development.

5. Seasonal Improvements

Raised beds, pollinator plantings, woodland understory development, meadow refinement, and patio integration can be implemented over time using the same transparent labor and materials formula.
