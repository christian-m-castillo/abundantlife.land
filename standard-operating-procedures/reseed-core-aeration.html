Standard Operating Procedure — Tall Fescue Reseeding

1. Site Preparation

  1. Mow: Set mower height to 2–2.5 inches. Bag and remove clippings from the lawn.
  2. Inspect for thatch: If the thatch layer is thicker than ¼ inch, use a dethatcher or rake to remove it in affected areas.
  3. Core Aeration: Use a powered core aerator. Cover the entire lawn in overlapping passes. Cores should be 2–3 inches deep and spaced every 3–4 inches.
  4. Rake lightly: Use a leaf rake to break up soil plugs and expose soil for seed-to-soil contact.

2. Seeding & Finishing

  1. Seed: Measure and spread 50 lbs of premium tall fescue seed evenly over the 4,600 sq ft area (approx. 10–11 lbs per 1,000 sq ft).
  2. Top-dress: Apply a light 1/8" layer of compost or planting mix. Target ~1.8 cubic yards for full coverage.
  3. Starter fertilizer: Apply a balanced, slow-release starter fertilizer according to label rate (~1 lb nitrogen per 1,000 sq ft).
  4. Rake-in: Gently rake seeded areas to lightly incorporate seed into the soil surface.

3. Watering Plan

  1. Days 0–14: Water lightly 2–3 times daily to keep soil surface moist (not soggy).
  2. Days 15–42: After germination begins, reduce to once daily deep watering, then transition to 2–3 times weekly, ½–1 inch each watering.
  3. After establishment: Maintain 1–1.5 inches of water weekly, including rainfall.

4. Mowing & Maintenance

  1. First mow: When seedlings reach ~3.5 inches. Set mower height at 2.5 inches.
  2. Subsequent mows: Continue at 2.5 inches for the first 3 mowings, then gradually raise to 3–4 inches for long-term health.
  3. Avoid traffic: Keep people and pets off new seedlings for at least 2 weeks after germination.

5. Follow-Up & Inspection

  1. Inspection: Return 14–21 days after seeding to check germination rate and overall lawn health.
  2. Adjustments: Recommend overseeding, extra fertilizer, or irrigation changes if needed.

6. Safety & Professional Standards

  • Wear PPE: safety glasses, gloves, ear protection (when operating machinery).
  • Operate equipment according to manufacturer’s guidelines.
  • Dispose of debris (clippings, thatch) neatly and leave site clean.
  • Communicate with the client about watering, mowing, and care instructions before leaving.
Reminder: Success depends on both proper installation and homeowner care. Always leave written watering and mowing instructions with the client.
